How do I pay my tax bill?

  • We accept money orders, cash and personal checks.
  • Credit cards are accepted here online.
  • Partial payments are also accepted.
  • Payments for City Taxes can be paid at the City Hall, 161 E Grand River Ave., Williamston, MI 48895.
  • Review our coin acceptance policy

    • What do I do in change of property owndership?

    Ownership changes are officially recorded with the County Register of Deeds, after which copies of these deeds are provided to the assessor for record-keeping. Subsequently, the assessor forwards these updates to the treasurer. However, the treasurer receives these updates only periodically, typically a few times a year when tax bills are printed and mailed out.

    For any necessary corrections related to property ownership, please contact the assessor, Pete Preston, directly at 517-655-2448.

    Why didn't I recieve my tax bill?

    Sometimes owner address changes aren’t received before tax bills are prepared. Call 517-655-2774 to resolve any incorrect information.

    When do taxes become delinquent?

    Property taxes that remain unpaid become delinquent and are transferred to the County Treasurer on March 1. At that point, additional penalties and fees may be applied. All inquiries and payments regarding delinquent property taxes must be directed to the Ingham County Treasurer’s office. For further information, please contact the County Treasurer’s office at 517-676-7220.

    What happens if I don't pay my business "personal property" taxes?

    Failure to pay personal property taxes can lead to the forfeiture of the respective personal property to the local government entity.

    What if I think I already paid my tax bill?

    If you possess a cancelled check or receipt indicating your payment, please contact me promptly. Occasionally, taxpayers may overlook a summer or winter bill, or fail to realize they have paid late and accrued penalties.

    My taxes are escrowed, why did I recieve a tax bill?

    This is the taxpayer copy. Additionally, we have sent a copy to your Mortgage Company or financial institution if a number is indicated next to "Mort Code." If you've recently switched lending institutions, please provide a copy of this statement to your tax-paying agent. Your agent can then request additional statements from our office as needed.

    Why didn't I receive a copy of my paid tax reciept?

    If you require a receipt for your tax bill, kindly provide a stamped self-addressed envelope. Alternatively, you can access this information on the City’s website.
